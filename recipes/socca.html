Socca

Description

Farinata, socca, torta di ceci, or cecina is a type of thin, unleavened pancake or crÃªpe made from chickpea flour. It originated in Genoa and later became a typical food of the Ligurian Sea coast, from Nice to Sardinia and Elba islands. It is also typical in Gibraltar, where it is called calentita.

Ingredients

  • 50cl water
  • 6 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • pepper
  • 250g chickpea flour

Steps

  1. Provide two plates of about 40 cm in diameter or one of 70 cm.
  2. In a deep bowl, put the cold water and whisk in the chickpea flour.
  3. Add two tablespoons of olive oil and a teaspoon of fine salt. Mix vigorously to remove lumps. If necessary, sift.
  4. The batter should have the consistency of whole milk. If it is too thick, add a little water and mix again.
  5. Preheat your oven on maximum position for 10 minutes.
  6. On one or two circular plates, pour the contents of four tablespoons of olive oil. Divide and bake for 5 minutes.
  7. Remove the baking sheet from the oven, pour the preparation and spread it evenly.
  8. Bake immediately, in the top of the oven.
  9. After 2 minutes, turn the thermostat to the grill position. Leave to cook for 5 to 7 minutes, so that the crust is golden brown, even a little burnt in places... During cooking, you can pierce the blisters that form with a knife.
  10. Remove from the oven, cut and serve, making sure to season with plenty of pepper.
